#  Cracking the Medication Administration Code: Safe Practices for the Australian OSCE

Title :
Cracking the Medication Administration Code: Safe Practices for the Australian OSCE
NOV 20
Medication administration is a critical OSCE station. To succeed, follow
these principles:
The 6 Rights – Right patient, right medication, right dose, right time,
right route and right documentation. Always check identifiers and
document accurately.
Manage Allergies and Contraindications – Ask about allergies, review
drug histories and check for interactions. Clarify unclear orders before
administering medications.
Use Aseptic Technique – Perform hand hygiene, wear appropriate PPE and
maintain a clean field.
Handle Complex Medications – Learn how to draw up and administer
insulin, anticoagulants and other high‑risk drugs. Double‑check
calculations and observe patients for adverse reactions.
Provide Patient Education – Explain medication purpose, potential side
effects and what to do if adverse reactions occur.
Practicing these steps will help you administer medications safely and
confidently during the OSCE.
